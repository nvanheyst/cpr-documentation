Once you have opened Husky's top, the first thing you see is the _Electronics Tray_.
This tray includes the:

* Husky's _System Interface Assembly_ circuit board
* Primary computer
* IMU
* Optional secondary computer or graphics card, _(not shown in the image)_
* Optional USB hub, _(not shown in the image)_

You may want to remove the _Electronics Tray_ to access the integration bay under the tray,
  or to maintain Husky's _Batteries_ and _Power Distribution_ components. 

#### Hinging The Electronics Tray

<center>
  <figure>
    <img
      src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_electronics_tray_2.png"
      width="600"
    />
    <figcaption>Tray Hinged, And Then Held With A Prop Rod</figcaption>
  </figure>
</center>

<details>
  <summary>To hinge the tray:</summary>

  1.  Shut off the _Circuit Breaker_
  2.  Remove the _Top Plate_ or _Enclosure_ from the Husky
  3.  Disconnect the motor cables, _(2 per motor, so 8 cables total)_
  4.  Disconnect the computer's Ethernet cable from the network switch 
  5.  Disconnect the large orange connector near the rear-right of the robot.
      This connector provides main power, and mobility power to the _Electronics Tray_.
  6.  Remove the tray's 2 front screws
  7.  Loosen the tray's 2 rear screws, _(these will become the tray's hinge pin)_
  8.  Lift the front of the tray

  :::note

  When reinstalling the tray:

  * Torque the tray's 4 screws to 3 N·m.

  :::

</details>

:::danger Exposed +V Batteries

Lifting the tray will expose Husky's batteries.
The batteries's exposed +V terminals are always live, even when Husky's circuit breaker is in the Off position.
The batteries are fused, but can still produce an electric arc if shorted to ground.

Wear protective equipment to guard your skin and eyes.

Keep the workspace clean, without any combustible materials nearby.

Review your workplace's safety procedures related to fires of LiFePO<sub>4</sub> batteries.

:::

#### Removing The Electronics Tray

<center>
  <figure>
    <img
      src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_electronics_tray_3.png"
      width="600"
    />
    <figcaption>Tray Removed, But Still Tethered By The USB And HDMI Cables</figcaption>
  </figure>
</center>

<details>
  <summary>To remove the tray:</summary>

  1.  Return the tray to the level position, _(not hinged)_
  2.  Take pictures of all the cables and connections to the _System Interface Assembly_.
      The cables have labels with names that match the PCBA's silkscreen, but pictures will help you reinstall these cables later.
  3.  <details>
        <summary>Disconnect all the cables, _(listed from the left side of the robot, towards the right)_:</summary>
        1.  _(Any cables from your custom integration)_
        2.  Front Ethernet cable to the network switch, _(which should have been removed in the previous step)_
        3.  2X Wi-Fi, _(plastic bracket's W1 and W2)_
        4.  Current Sensor, _(BATTERY I)_
        5.  HMI Display, _(USER INTERFACE)_
        6.  Battery CANbus, _(CAN_BAT)_
        7.  2X Status Lights, _(STATUS 1, STATUS 2)_
        8.  2X Large VBAT Contactors, _(UP CTCR1, UP CTCR2)_
        9.  2X Shunt Brake Contactors, _(AUX/SB CTCR1, AUX/SB CTCR2)_
        10. 2X Mobility Contactors, _(M CTCR1, M CTCR2)_
        11. 4X Fans, _(FAN 1, FAN 2, FAN 3, FAN 4)_
        12. Optional, Wireless Emergency Stop Cable, _(W ES)_
        13. Wireless Emergency Stop Disable Keyswitch, _(W ES BYP)_
        14. 2X Emergency Stop Buttons, _(ES1, ES2)_
        15. Limit Switch, _(CHRG INTL)_
        16. Network Switch Power, _(SYS4)_
        17. Main Ground, _(GND)_
        18. Low Battery Shutdown, _(CB KILL)_
        19. Main Contactor, _(MAIN CTCR)_
        20. System Standby Power, _(VSTBY)_
        21. Tray Bond, _(Tray's threaded stud, without identification)_
        22. Leave the USB cable connected
        23. Leave the HDMI cable connected
        24. Large orange connector _(V MOT and V SYS, which should have been removed in the previous step)_
      </details>

  4.  Remove the tray's 2 rear screws
  5.  Lift the tray out of the Husky.
      Note that the tray is still tethered to the Husky with a USB cable and a HDMI cable.
      Rotate the tray, and set it behind the Husky.
  6.  Disconnect the USB and HDMI cables from the Husky's Debug HMI area.
