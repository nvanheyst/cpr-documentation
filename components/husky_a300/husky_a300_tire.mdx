### Tire and Inner Tube  {#tire-maintenance}

[//]: <> (TODO, add a hero shot of the wheel)
[//]: <> (TODO, make images with transparent background)

:::safety-caution

Place the robot up on blocks prior to replacing the tire or inner tube.
The robot may become tip over if you remove a wheel without stabilizing the chassis.

:::

<br/>

<details>
  <summary>To replace the tire and inner tube:</summary>

  1.  Shut off the robot's circuit breaker.

  2.  Remove the six M8×1.25 screws holding the wheel to the motor.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_1.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  3.  Deflate the tire by pressing on the Schrader valve's core.

  4.  Remove the six M4×0.7 screws and nuts that hold the split rim together.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_2.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  5.  Remove the outer rim.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_3.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  6.  Remove the tire and tube from the inner rim.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_4.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  7.  Replace the tire and/or tube as needed with new parts.
      Make sure the tire's chevron treads are facing the correct direction, as the left and right wheels have different tread orientations.
      Ensure that the tube is installed in the tire and inflated only far enough that it takes its shape.
      Inflating further will make reassembly difficult.

  8.  Place the tire—_(with inner tube)_—onto the inner rim, aligning the valve stem with the notch in the rim.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_5.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  9.  Place the outer rim on top, aligning the value stem with the notch in the rim.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_6.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  10. Reinstall the six M4×0.7 screws and nuts, tightening them in a star pattern to 2 N·m.

      :::note

      It may be necessary to use three longer M4×0.7 screws on a temporary basis to help pull the inner and outer rims together and ensure alignment,
        allowing the first three standard M4 screws to be installed.
      Then, the temporary M4×0.7 screws can be removed and the standard ones installed.
      As you tighten the screws, ensure that the valve stem is not being pinched.

      :::

      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_7.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  11. Inflate the tire to 140 kPa _(20 psi)_.

  12. Place the wheel on the motor and reinstall the six M8×1.25 screws, tightening them in a star pattern to 20 N·m.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_tire_1.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>
      
</details>
