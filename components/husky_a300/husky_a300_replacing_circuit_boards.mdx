<br/>

Husky has several circuit boards—_(PCBAs)_.
It is uncommon for a PCBA to fail during operation, but could occur from physical damage during a maintenance task.
If a failure occurs, usually only one board needs to be replaced, rather than the full _System Interface Assembly_.

<Admonition type="danger" title="ESD Protection">
  PCBAs are sensitive components and can be damaged easily through electrostatic discharge—_(ESD)_.
  Be sure to follow proper ESD protection measures prior to handling any circuit boards.
</Admonition>

:::safety-warning

Confirm that Husky's Emergency Stop System is functioning after changing any of the circuit boards.

:::

<details>
  <summary>Procedure for replacing the DC-DC and BLDC circuit boards</summary>

  1.  Turn off the robot's circuit breaker.
  
  2.  Remove the 7 screws that fasten the _Top Plate_ to the chassis and remove the _Top Plate_.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_1.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  3.  Remove the cable brush holder.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_2.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  4.  Remove the heat shield covering the DC-DC or BLDC circuit boards.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_3.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  5.  Remove the failing circuit board by gently lifting the board up.
      These circuit boards have many pins, so a large amount of force is required. 
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_4.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  6.  Install the new circuit board by aligning the connector and gently sliding it into place.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_5.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  7.  Reinstall the heat shields.

  8.  Reinstall the cable brush holder.

  9.  Put the _Top Plate_ back in place and finger tighten the 7 screws.
      Note that the screw with the sealing washer goes in the inner location, and the remaining 6 screws are installed around the perimeter.
      Tighten all screws to 3 N·m.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_6.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Procedure for replacing the Common Core circuit board</summary>

  1.  Turn off the robot's circuit breaker.

  2.  Remove the 7 screws that fasten the _Top Plate_ to the chassis and remove the _Top Plate_.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_1.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  3.  Remove the Common Core by removing the 2 retaining screws, pushing out on the retaining clips, and then gently pulling the board out horizontally.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_7.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  4.  Install the new circuit board by gently sliding it into place horizontally,
        ensure that the retaining clips are secured, and that the two screws have been installed.
  
  5.  Put the _Top Plate_ back in place and finger tighten the 7 screws.
      Note that the screw with the sealing washer goes in the inner location, and the remaining 6 screws are installed around the perimeter.
      Tighten all screws to 3 N·m.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_6.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Procedure for replacing the Main circuit board</summary>

  1.  Turn off the robot's circuit breaker.

  2.  Remove the 7 screws that fasten the _Top Plate_ to the chassis and remove the _Top Plate_.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_1.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>

  3. Disconnect all cables from the Main circuit board.
    <center>
      <img
        src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_8.jpg"
        width="400"
      />
    </center>

  4.  Remove the Main circuit board by removing the screws and then lifting out the board.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_9.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>

  5.  Place the old and new Main circuit boards on an ESD mat.

  6.  Move the functional DC-DC, BLDC, and Common Core circuit boards from the old Main circuit board to the new Main circuit board by following the procedures above.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_10.jpg"
          width="400"
        />
      </center>
  
  7.  Install the new Main circuit board tighten the retaining screws to 1 N·m.

  8.  Reconnect all cables, noting that the labels on the cables should match those on the board.
  
  9.  Put the _Top Plate_ back in place and finger tighten the 7 screws.
      Note that the screw with the sealing washer goes in the inner location, and the remaining 6 screws are installed around the perimeter.
      Tighten all screws to 3 N·m.
      <center>
        <img
          src="/img/robot_images/husky_a300_images/husky_a300_circuitboard_6.jpg"
          width="600"
        />
      </center>
</details>
