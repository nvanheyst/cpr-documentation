### Batteries

  <figure>

The Husky's batteries are visible once you remove the _Electronics Tray_.
The batteries +V terminals are always live, even when the circuit breaker is in the Off position.
You may want to remove the batteries to replace them, or to access the _Power Distribution_ subassembly.

:::note

The images show the Husky 40 Ah configuration which has 2 batteries.
The 80 Ah and 120 Ah configurations follow a similar process, but with 4 or 6 batteries.

:::

:::info

Husky's batteries should last 3000 charge cycles if they are charge balanced at least once per week, and not left in a depleted state.

If you order new batteries from Clearpath Robotics, ask us to configure the batteries for a Husky A300.
We will configure the battery software addresses and CAN address before shipping them to you.

:::

  3.  Remove the plastic covers from the fuse holders.
      These are always live voltage, up to +30 VDC.

      You may also need to cut a cable tie.

      When reinstalling the busbar:

      * Torque the nuts to 3 N·m.

      :::

  5.  Lift the red cable and busbar away from the batteries.
      The cable will not be live, once it is removed from the fuse holders.
      The fuses will still be positive voltage.

      <center>

  7.  Remove the screw and black cable.

      When reinstalling the cable:

      * Torque the screw to 5 N·m.

  8.  Remove the screw and ground busbar.

      When reinstalling the busbar:

  :::note When Reinstalling Busbars

  The original Husky A300 builds in 2025 used bare copper busbars.
  Joints with bare copper surfaces should include Penetrox&trade; between the mating faces.
  Excess Penetrox&trade; can be removed after the joint has been fastened.

  Joints with tin-tin contacts do not require Penetrox&trade;.
